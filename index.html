Student Council

For the 2019 Brantford Collegiate Institute Student Council Semi-Formal, our council decided to go with the theme of Mardi Gras. On top of organizing the event for over 400 students, scheduling over 50 staff, placing orders for food vendors, hiring DJs, and setting up the photobooth, I also designed the promotional posters, social media posts, and tickets. The designs were well received and thoroughly praised for their aesthetic use of thematic images and eye-catching glitter effects.

Highlight: Attendance at event was increased by 35%

Cross Cuts Festival

The Cross Cuts Festival is local festival that attracts entries from all over the Grand Erie District School Board. In addition to planning the judging process, I designed the promotional materials and posters for the festival in 2018 as well as submitted entries in the photography categories in both 2018 and 2019.

Highlight: Designs attracted more sponsors in following year and I was awarded 1st, 2nd, and 3rd within the School Life, Open, and Digital Editing categories respectively.

Young Artists' Recital

Run by the Brant Music Club, the Young Artists' Recital is an annual celebration that showcases the talent of local youth. In 2019, I was fortunate enough to not only have the opportunity to design a poster for the festival, but to also perform within it!

Highlight: Members of the Brant Music Club were so impressed by the quality of my work that they publicly congratulated me in the opening ceremonies

HoliDays and Spirit Weeks

As the Co-President of my high school's student council, not only was I responsible for planning events and dances, coordinating the 55 members of my team, delegating responsibilities to the staff, and preparing agendas for meetings, but I also represented the ideal of school spirit as well! To support this goal, I implemented seasonal spirit weeks and curated engaging posters for these events to foster a more relaxed and welcoming atmosphere within my school.

Highlight: Spirit weeks were widely praised by students and staff alike as one of the best recent additions to the school calendar

DailyUI

As a summer personal project, I decided to participate in the DailyUI Challenge, an email service which sends you a new user interface prompt to design everyday. Along with some other designers from my high school, we decided to assign an hour of every day to take on this challenge and improve the efficiency of our ideation, wireframing, prototyping, and illustration processes. Thinking Playground

For Thinking Playground, a startup that designs innovative puzzles which develop a child's ability to problem solve, I curated an effective brand direction based off user personas. Equipped with this knowledge, I illustrated an appropriate logo and helped design mockups for the packaging of their sudoku product.

Highlight: Owner of the company was so impressed by my thorough advice that I was asked to help with future endeavours

Yearbook Cover

As the graphic design lead of the Brantford Collegiate Institute Yearbook Club from 2017-2019, I was responsible for the creation of both yearbook covers. Since this was often the part of the yearbook that had to be finalized before the rest of the team could start curating the content and photos, I was often forced to think creatively under intense time pressure. Thankfully, with clever time management and excellent organizational abilities, I was able to not only create yearbook covers before the deadline, but produce engaging, thematically-appropriate, and complex designs to accompany them.

Highlight: Many of the senior staff commented that these yearbook covers were among their favourite designs since they've been at the school

Homecoming and Mustang Day

As two of the largest traditions at Brantford Collegiate Institute, Homecoming and Mustang Day demanded a large amount of attention and promotion. Fortunately, I was able to manage to achieve both. Even while balancing difficult senior-level classes with daily piano and jazz band practices, I was able to effectively prioritize my tasks and revolutionize these two events. On top of adding a photobooth to Homecoming and introducing new activites in the day-long, school-wide competition of Mustang Day, I was able to creatively utilize my graphic design abilities to create the necessary posters, geofilters, social media campaigns, and tickets to make both of these traditions a massive success!

Highlight: Increased attendance at both of these events by 25%

Major League Hacking Learn Day

To raise awareness about programming amongst students back at my home town, I organized a Major League Hacking Learn Day. This included workshops that introduced students to web apps, messaging bots, and game design. To raise the amount of people who may be inspired by this event, I designed a poster to be hung up and posted on social media throughout the school board!

HEROS

HEROS, or Health Educators Reaching Other Students, is a youth advocacy group at the Brant County Health Unit. Whether it be hosting movie nights to advocate for the removal of smoking in youth-rated movies, organizing an art gallery to promote plain and standardized packaging, or hosting discussions about the dangers of e-cigarettes, this group has provided me with an outlet for my passion for the well-being of my community. In addition to utilizing my graphic design abilities to create an effective outreach on social media, I have also individually worked with members on their graphic design and public speaking to ensure that there will be a strong foundation after I leave.

Highlight: Through our group and many others across Canada, we were successful in our Plain and Standardized Packaging campaign; in May 2018, Bill S-5 implemented restrictions on brand colours, logos, and graphics on tobacco packages.
